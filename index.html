Topic

Our group ‘Newb Entity’ has decided to make a web application. This application allows the users to generate their own Dungeons and Dragons campaign.The app’s main feature is the Campaign Builder, which allows users to generate a Dungeons & Dragons campaign based on the user’s inputs. The user can choose the setting and theme of the campaign, as well as the level of the adventurers who will be carrying out the campaign. The speed and flexibility of the Campaign Builder makes it a powerful tool in the hands of any Dungeon Master, whether they be new to D&D or a seasoned veteran.

Additional features such as a D&D focused social interaction platform give this project of ours the potential to connect D&D players from around the world. They would be encouraged to share their experiences, stories and expertise to any new players who want to become a part of the fascinating world of Dungeons & Dragons. The ultimate goal of this project is to simplify campaign creation, and to connect adventurers of all levels of experience to unite them in a single, purpose built online community.

The biggest motivation that has encouraged our group to create such an online program, comes from our team’s passion in Dungeons and Dragons. Due to our team’s interest in Dungeons and Dragons, and the get togethers our members do every week, has kept us all motivated and excited to bring this wonderful project to the D&D community, it is because of this reason that this project is important to our group.

Our groups project fits in with current IT trends, because, there is a constant rise of Dungeons and Dragons players, and having such a huge fanbase, D&D will always have new players, experienced players as well as returning players, who are trying to find a new and easier way to learn the game and connect to the rest of the community. If a future employer was to examine our project, it would show that we the ability to work as a team to create a project that will benefit people all around the world.

landscape

If our research is correct, then our groups idea of building an actual campaign generator that takes users inputs to generate a complete campaign, is a one of a kind project. Due to designing an online Dungeons and Dragons community, there are bound to be competitors, however, because of the function of our project, we believe, that our website will stand out from all the rest, and that will help draw in the community.

Detailed Description

Aims

The aim for DnD campaign generator is to provide a dungeon master with a 5th edition adventure for them to run with either their friends or family. You can either be a very experienced dungeon master or a Beginner. This tool will give you the basic information needed to set out on your very own adventure. It also gives you randomised monsters that you will be able to encounter to spice that game night you might have planned.

One goal we have for our project is more settings and themes for the campaign generator. One thing I find hard when trying to figure out where I want to make the campaign/adventure is, do I want it in a harsh rugged mountain top? Or maybe a lush and vibrant rain forest? Who knows that’s up to the person using the application. That’s why this goal will be to make sure that there is something that will make your imagination run wild. In the future we want to see features added in that lets you pick multiply themes. So that once you pass through that mountain, they might end up in a forest surrounded by tall trees and lakes to passing though towns and cities. We would like to see our project grow like the adventures the players will take. Other goal for this project our group has had in mind is adding items into the mix. Being able to add all the 5th edition items from the game into our application, would see players being able add items to Their playable characters and even for the dungeon master to assign loot for defeating monsters or solving puzzles and riddles. In addition to items it would be able to assign currency to players and monsters. Giving the dungeon master a tool to distribute currency to players or working out how much gold that dragon was hoarding.

A very distant goal for our application would be the dungeon/map maker. It would be a tool that allows you to create a landscape for your adventure, it would have such feature like;

  • Gridlines to give a distance guide,
  • Ability to add walls and doors,
  • Add monsters and obsticles,
  • Add items like chests of treasure,
  • Add non playable characters (NPC's),
  • Add buildings and paths and,
  • Add trees and boulders to navigate.

    • This visual tool would be a great addition to our application to get the user immersed into what they are creating and give a visual guide to help the dungeon master navigate the adventure they want their players to take.

    Tools and Technologies

    The front end of our application revolves around React 16.8.6, Redux 4.0.1, HTML 5, ECMA Script 10, and Sass-loader 7.1.0, which is all consolidated using Webpack 4.29.6 with plugins like @babel/core 7.4.0 that allow our application to use modern JavaScript features and transform them into backwards compatible polyfills older web browsers can understand. A /src folder in the application's root is converted into bundles and moved into a /dist folder to be served by Node.

    The back end uses Node 8.11.4 and Express 4.16.4. This backend uses Axios 0.19.0 to make HTTP requests to a third-party API called dnd5eapi.co to return game-specific datasets like monsters, items, and spells in JSON [1]. When the user loads the UI, the UI makes its first get request to the DND API and stores the results in the application's state. No database is necessary, nor is local storage or cookies needed.

    The application is hosted on Heroku for free and hits the 1 second page-load benchmark without further optimizations necessary. The reason this application loads so quickly is because it defaults to a compression type developed by Google called Brotli, and should this not be supported, it sends GZipped files instead [2].

    We can see that the entire application is placed into an iframe and that the compressed file size of the bundles that power this app are only 83.85kbs compared to a total file size of 440.18kbs uncompressed. That's an 80.95% decrease in file size, which is absolutely amazing technology. All of the tools and technology that power this application are free and production ready. This application is so light weight that a Heroku free account packs more than enough punch to keep up with requests; however, hosting will need to be scaled as use increases.

    I have hosted applications on Heroku in the past using similar technology stacks, and I've always been pleased by ease of use, performance, and value for money.

    [1]"D&D 5th Edition API", Dnd5eapi.co, 2019. [Online]. Available: http://www.dnd5eapi.co/docs/. [Accessed: 11- Nov- 2019].

    [2]"Brotli vs Gzip Compression. How we improved our latency by 37%", Medium, 2019. [Online]. Available: https://medium.com/oyotech/how-brotli-compression-gave-us-37-latency-improvement-14d41e50fee4. [Accessed: 11- Nov- 2019].

    Testing

    This application has already undergone informal Ad Hoc testing [3] where I, the developer, have intentionally attempted to break the application. This resulted in the discovery of bugs early on that I was able to fix before the rest of the team had the chance to experience them. Likewise, I have done rudimentary usability testing, and the application has been used and tested by 7 other people, which is okay coverage at this point of prototyping. In the future, I will write proper unit tests using a framework / test runner called Jest [4]. This will enable me to write basic assertions. For example, I would expect that when the /getMonsters route on the server is contacted, it will return an object containing three arrays that each contain three monsters, and I would expect that after clicking the Generate Campaign button that #campaignText and #campaignHeader divs are not blank.

    This application already supports linting using the stringent guidelines [5] established by Airbnb’s development team. Implementing both unit testing and a uniform code style will facilitate higher quality software with fewer bugs. Finally, I would make a call out and offer a bug bounty to any security consultant who can successfully hack our server. Although we do not hold a user's personal information in any form, it still wouldn't be ideal if our server were to be rooted and defaced.

    Risks

    The single biggest risk of this project is relying on a third-party API to serve application-critical information. This is easy to solve. dnd5eapi.co API can be cloned directly from the GitHub repository, and their entire database self-hosted. I would be deploying a copy to Azure and only redirecting requests to this resource if a request to dnd5eapi.co should fail, which allows us to save on deployment costs while still covering our bases in a failure.

    Additionally, there is a chance Heroku could go down. I would have to write a client-side script that tests if our Heroku-hosted server is accessible, and if it's not, use a different server hosted on Microsoft Azure. Again, not using paid resources as our primary resources will keep costs down. I don't foresee our users exceeding the limitations of Heroku's free resources for quite some time, but I will have to monitor traffic closely and provision additional resources as demand increases.

    Regarding development time, there is quite a lot of core features left to implement. The existing application is still a toy, and there's a significant amount of creative writing time in addition to writing the code itself, so it would be prudent to unload some of the creative writing to the core team as well as explore hiring freelance writers; this will mitigate the risk of the lead developer burning out. Finally, the risk with every project is that the project will not get off the ground. Given this project can be implemented for zero cost, the only thing we stand to lose is time and invested emotions should this project fail to gain traction, but at the very least, we all gain a nice portfolio piece to decorate our resumes.

    Group Process and Communications

    Group communication occurs daily over Discord in both a formal and informal context. We're not just colleagues, but also friends. This has both advantages and disadvantages. Working with friends can be nice for morale and comradery, but it's also difficult to reprimand a friend if that becomes necessary. Our group has already shown more than adequate conflict resolution skills despite an informal working environment, having worked together in previous assignments. We have had difficulties with group members taking extended periods of absence without explanation, and we've dealt with this by making contact as many times as necessary to get a response.

    Moving forward, it would be wise to establish additional channels of communication. An exchange of mobile numbers would allow us to reduce response time should a team member become occupied with other obligations. Formal allocation of tasks with expected deadlines is another strategy we could implement to better guarantee every group member understands their obligations and has a clear task to work towards. In the past, tasks have been distributed on a volunteer basis, but this might not be the most effective approach because it's too easy to get distracted by other obligations and end up with contribution imbalance that adversely affects the team. When meetings are absolutely essential and need to be planned, the approach we use is to designate a day and time that's mutually convenient and show up. This has been more than adequate for our group.
